Phoenix, the capital of Arizona, was incorporated in 1881. Located in the south-central region of the state, it is home to several major attractions in the United States, such as South Mountain Park and Preserve, which spans 16,000 acres, and the Desert Botanical Garden, which contains one of the largest collections of desert plants. The population of Phoenix has dramatically increased in recent years; it is now ranked the 7th most popular city for relocation, with a population of 1,608,139 according to the most recent census. The city is predominantly urban, with some suburban areas, and the median household income is $72,092.

Tucson was incorporated in 1877 and has a population of 542,629. Located in the southeastern portion of the state, the city is predominantly urban, with some suburban areas. The median household income is $64,323. Tucson is surrounded by five mountain ranges: the Catalina, Rincon, Santa Rita, Tucson, and Tortolita Mountains, offering abundant opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to enjoy hiking and rock climbing. The city is also known for its sunshine, boasting 300 days of sun each year. Additionally, Tucson is a popular destination for stargazers, thanks to its high elevation, clear skies, and low light pollution.

Pinetop-Lakeside defies the common myth that Arizona is only desert and cactus. Home to 3 million acres of forests, meadows, mesas, lakes, streams, and brushland, it is truly a scenic wonder. Incorporated in 1984, the town has a population of 4,557 and is located on Arizona's eastern border, offering a mix of suburban and rural living. Despite its small population, the area is home to over 400 species of wildlife. However, it also hosts dangerous animals such as bears, wolves, and mountain lions. Additionally, there are over 200 miles of developed multi-use trails, ideal for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Did you know?

The Arizona National Scenic Trail encompasses 800 miles stretching from the U.S Mexico Border, the entire state of Arizona, to the border of Utah. The trail has areas friendly for hikers, runners, or bikers, with challenges for the inexperienced or the experienced alike. According to USDA.gov, the trail "has something for everyone". Showing off diverse vegetation, wildlife, canyons, deserts, mountains, history, and scenary.
