About Arizona

A huge misconception about Arizona is that it is completely desert everywhere you go. However, there are actually six different major biomes that can be found in the state: desert, grassland, chaparral, woodland, forest, and tundra. Although the state of Arizona does not touch the coast, it ranks third in biome diversity, following California and Texas. Arizona is home to 22 national parks, 33 state parks, and 18 national monuments. It is also home to the only Wonder of the World located in the United States: The Grand Canyon.

Traveling in Arizona can come with huge risks, please come prepared for your visit!
