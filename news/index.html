One Of The Grand Canyon's Deadliest Years

This year, at least 16 people have died at the Grand Canyon, with causes ranging from boating accidents to drownings and falls. While extreme weather and risky behavior have contributed to recent fatalities, park officials emphasize that the August surge in deaths is an anomaly, not the norm. Staff shortages and climate change's impact on water levels are increasing challenges, especially as visitors face hazards like flash floods and punishing heat.
